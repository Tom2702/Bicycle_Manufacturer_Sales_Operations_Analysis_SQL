## 3. Background & Overview

This project analyzes sales, production, purchasing, and customer retention performance for a bicycle manufacturer using the AdventureWorks 2019 dataset in BigQuery.

The analysis focuses on product subcategory performance, year-over-year growth, sales territory ranking, seasonal discount cost, customer retention, stock movement, stock-to-sales ratio, and purchase order status.

## 4. Business Questions

This project is designed to answer the following questions:

1. Which product subcategories generated the highest quantity, sales value, and order volume in the last 12 months?
2. Which subcategories had the highest year-over-year growth rate?
3. Which territories ranked in the top 3 by order quantity each year?
4. How much seasonal discount cost was generated by each subcategory?
5. What was the customer retention pattern for successfully shipped orders in 2014?
6. How did product stock levels change month over month in 2011?
7. What was the stock-to-sales ratio by product and month in 2011?
8. How many purchase orders were pending in 2014 and what was their total value?

## 5. Dataset Description

This project uses the `adventureworks2019` dataset in BigQuery.

Main tables used:

| Table | Description |
| --- | --- |
| `Sales.SalesOrderDetail` | Sales order line items, order quantity, product ID, line total, and special offer ID |
| `Sales.SalesOrderHeader` | Order-level data such as customer, territory, order status, and order date |
| `Production.Product` | Product details and product subcategory mapping |
| `Production.ProductSubcategory` | Product subcategory names |
| `Sales.SpecialOffer` | Discount type and discount percentage |
| `Production.WorkOrder` | Production and stock quantity data |
| `Purchasing.PurchaseOrderHeader` | Purchase order status and total value |

## 8. SQL Techniques

The project uses the following SQL techniques:

1. Common Table Expressions (CTEs) to structure multi-step logic.
2. Date functions such as `DATE`, `DATE_SUB`, `EXTRACT`, and `FORMAT_DATE`.
3. Aggregation functions such as `SUM` and `COUNT(DISTINCT ...)`.
4. Window functions such as `LAG` and `DENSE_RANK`.
5. `SAFE_DIVIDE` to avoid division-by-zero errors.
6. `COALESCE` to handle missing stock or sales values.
7. `FULL OUTER JOIN` to keep unmatched stock and sales records.

## 9. Conclusion

In the last 12-month result, Tires and Tubes had the highest order volume, while Helmets and Jerseys contributed strong sales value. Mountain Frames, Socks, and Road Frames showed the strongest YoY quantity growth.

Territory 4 consistently ranked first by order quantity from 2011 to 2014, followed by territories 6 and 1. Seasonal discount cost was concentrated in Helmets and increased from 2012 to 2013.

Customer retention dropped sharply after the first purchase month. Stock levels changed significantly month over month, and several products had sales quantities close to or slightly above stock quantities. In 2014, there were 224 pending purchase orders with a total value of about 3.87M.

## 10. Recommendations

- Focus sales and inventory planning on high-growth subcategories such as Mountain Frames, Socks, and Road Frames.
- Study Territory 4 performance and apply successful sales practices to other territories.
- Review Helmet seasonal discounts to confirm whether a higher discount cost leads to profitable sales.
- Improve post-purchase engagement because customer retention declines strongly after M-0.
- Monitor products with large stock fluctuations to reduce shortage and overstock risk.
- Track pending purchase orders regularly to control procurement delays and cash flow.
